# Flag That - Levels Guide

Flag That has a **100-level progression system** organized into 6 tiers. Levels are purely a measure of mastery - all game modes and content are available from the start. Once you reach a level, you never lose it.

---

## Tiers

| Tier | Levels | Theme |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Starter** | 1-10 | Getting started with basic flag identification |
| **Explorer** | 11-20 | Building breadth, seeing more flags, trying modes |
| **Scholar** | 21-40 | Deepening mastery with accuracy and regions |
| **Expert** | 41-65 | Speed, harder modes, completionist goals |
| **Master** | 66-95 | Per-flag mastery, high accuracy, long streaks |
| **Legend** | 96-100 | Ultimate achievements |

## Game Modes Reference

These modes are referenced in level requirements:

- **Easy / Medium / Hard** - Standard flag identification at varying difficulty
- **Flash Flag** - Quick-fire flag recognition
- **Flag Puzzle** - Puzzle-style flag challenges
- **Time Attack** - Score as many correct answers as possible under time pressure
- **Neighbors** - Identify flags of neighboring countries
- **Impostor** - Spot the fake flag among real ones
- **Capital Connection** - Match flags with their country's capital

---

## Tips for Progressing

- **Play daily** to build your day streak (needed for levels 13, 23, 39, 56, 76, 87, 96)
- **Try every game mode** - you need all 9 modes played by level 94
- **Focus on regions** - Europe, Africa, Asia, Americas, and Oceania each have accuracy requirements
- **Speed matters** at higher levels - practice to get your answer time under 5 seconds
- **Earn badges** along the way - levels 30, 49, 72, 89, and 99 require specific badge counts
- **Consistency is key** - many levels require getting every flag right multiple times
- **The final level (100)** requires a 5-answer correct streak on every flag in the game
